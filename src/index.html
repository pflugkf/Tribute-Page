Isao Takahata

A Pioneer of Japanese Animation

Here is a timeline of Takahata-san's life:

  • 1935 - Isao Takahata is born in Ujiyamada (Ise), Mie Prefecture, Japan. He is the youngest of seven siblings.
  • 1945 - Takahata and his family survive a major US air raid on Okayama City. His experiences during this time would later act as inspiration for several sequences in his antiwar film, Grave of the Fireflies.
  • 1959 - Takahata graduates from the University of Tokyo with a degree in French literature and joins Toei Animation.
  • 1968 - Takahata directs his first animated feature film, The Great Adventure of Horus, Prince of the Sun, with Hayao Miyazaki as lead animator. The two become close friends.
  • 1985 - Miyazaki and Takahata co-found their own animation studio, Studio Ghibli. The name is inspired by the Italian term ghibli, adapted from the Arabic word for the hot desert winds. Miyazaki hoped that their new studio would "blow a new wind through the anime industry."
  • 1988 - Takahata's first Ghibli film, Grave of the Fireflies, is released alongside Miyazaki's My Neighbor Totoro. Both films receive critical acclaim, and the loveable Totoro becomes Studio Ghibli's mascot.
  • 1991 - Only Yesterday is released in theaters. The movie follows an officeworker reminiscing about her childhood as she takes a trip back to her rural hometown. An English-language version is not released until its 25th anniversary in 2016, leading some fans to refer to it as the "lost" Ghibli film.
  • 1994 - Pom Poko is released in theaters. It tells the story of a group of mythical creatures called tanuki trying to save their forest home from urban development.
  • 1999 - My Neighbors the Yamadas, a film adaptation of a popular Japanese comic strip, is released in theaters. A critical and commercial failure, Takahata decides to take on a more behind-the-scenes role at Studio Ghibli for a time.
  • 2013 - Takahata's last film, The Tale of Princess Kaguya, is released. Based on a classic Japanese folktale, it was nominated for Best Animated Feature at the 87th Academy Awards.
  • 2018 - Takahata dies of lung cancer on April 5th at a hospital in Tokyo. He was 82.

"Paku-san [Takahata's nickname], in those days we were living with all our might. You stood tall without bending to any hardships. This was an attitude we all shared. Thank you, Paku-san."

--Hayao Miyazaki at Isao Takahata's memorial service
